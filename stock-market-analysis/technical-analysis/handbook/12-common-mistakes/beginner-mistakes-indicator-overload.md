# Beginner Mistakes and Indicator Overload

## Common Beginner Mistakes

- **Trading without a plan:** Entering because “it looks good” or “someone said so.” No defined entry, exit, or stop. **Fix:** Write a trading plan; only trade when setup matches the plan.
- **No stop loss:** “I’ll hold until it comes back.” One bad trade can wipe a large part of the account. **Fix:** Every trade has a **stop** before entry; no exception.
- **Position too large:** Risking 5–10% or more per trade. A few losses in a row cause a big drawdown. **Fix:** Risk **0.5–2%** per trade (e.g. 1%).
- **Chasing:** Buying after a big move up (FOMO) or selling after a big move down (panic). **Fix:** Wait for **pullback** or **setup**; don’t chase.
- **Trading too many instruments or timeframes:** No focus; no edge in any one place. **Fix:** Trade **one or two** markets and **one or two** timeframes until you’re consistent.
- **Ignoring costs:** Spread and commission eat into small gains. **Fix:** Factor costs into your edge; prefer liquid, low-cost instruments for frequent trading.
- **No journal:** Repeating the same mistakes because you don’t track what went wrong. **Fix:** Log every trade (setup, result, emotional state) and review weekly.

## Indicator Overload

- **Problem:** Using **many** indicators (5+ MAs, RSI, MACD, Stochastic, Bollinger, etc.). They often **contradict** each other (one says buy, another says sell). Result: confusion, late entries, or no trades.
- **Why it happens:** Belief that “more information = better.” In practice, most indicators are **derived from price** and **lag**. Adding more doesn’t add independent information; it adds noise and delay.
- **Fix:** Use **few** indicators (e.g. one trend filter like EMA, one momentum like RSI or MACD). Or use **price action only** (structure, levels, candles). Define **one** clear set of conditions for entry; avoid “wait for all indicators to align.”

---

## Trading Psychology

- **Beginners** often **overestimate** their edge and **underestimate** variance. Expect **losing streaks**; size so you can survive them.
- **Indicator overload** is often a way to **feel safe** (“so many confirmations”). Real safety comes from **risk management** (size, stop) and **discipline** (following the plan), not from more lines on the chart.

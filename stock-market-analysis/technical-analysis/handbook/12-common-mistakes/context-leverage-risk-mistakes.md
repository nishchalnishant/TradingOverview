# Ignoring Context, Overleveraging, and Lack of Risk Management

## Ignoring Market Context

- **Problem:** Taking the same signal in **every** condition—e.g. buying every RSI < 30 without checking if the market is **trending down** (where RSI can stay low) or at **support** (where RSI oversold can mean bounce). Or trading **breakouts** in a choppy range where most breakouts fail.
- **Fix:** Always consider **context**: (1) **Trend** (uptrend, downtrend, range). (2) **Level** (support, resistance, structure). (3) **Timeframe** (higher TF bias; lower TF for entry). (4) **Session/volume** (e.g. avoid low-liquidity hours). Filter setups: e.g. only take long at support **in an uptrend** or after a **structure shift**.

---

## Overleveraging

- **Problem:** Using **high leverage** (e.g. 50:1 in forex, 10x in crypto) so that a **small** adverse move wipes the account or triggers margin call. One or two bad trades can mean total loss.
- **Fix:** Use **low or no leverage** when learning (e.g. 1:1 or 2:1). Even with experience, **cap** leverage (e.g. 5:1 max) and **size** position so that **max loss per trade** is still 1–2% of account. Leverage amplifies **both** gains and losses; it doesn’t create edge.

---

## Lack of Risk Management

- **Problems:** (1) **No fixed risk per trade**—sizing by “gut.” (2) **No max daily loss**—trading through a bad day and doubling losses. (3) **No max open risk**—several correlated positions all stop out together. (4) **Moving stops** further away when price goes against you (“hoping”). (5) **Adding to losers** to average down.
- **Fix:** (1) **Position size** from risk% and stop distance (see [08 — Position Sizing](../08-risk-management/position-sizing-and-risk-per-trade.md)). (2) **Stop trading** after losing e.g. 2% in a day. (3) Limit **number of open positions** and avoid over-concentration in one sector. (4) **Never** move stop against the trade; only to breakeven or to lock profit. (5) **Never** add to a losing position unless it’s part of a **predefined** scale-in plan (and even then, total risk must be capped).

---

## Chasing Trades

- **Problem:** Entering **after** a big move because of FOMO (e.g. buying the top of a pump). Usually results in poor entry and quick stop out.
- **Fix:** **Wait** for a pullback or a **new** setup. Define “valid entry” (e.g. at level, on confirmation); if you missed it, **skip** that trade. There will be other setups. “No trade” is a valid outcome.

---

## Summary

| Mistake | Fix |
|--------|-----|
| Ignoring context | Filter by trend, level, timeframe; don’t trade one signal in all conditions |
| Overleveraging | Use low leverage; size so 1 trade doesn’t blow the account |
| No risk management | Fixed risk% per trade; max daily loss; no moving stops against you; no adding to losers |
| Chasing | Wait for setup; accept “no trade” |

# Continuation Chart Patterns

Continuation patterns represent a **pause** or **consolidation** within a trend. The expectation is that price will **resume** in the **direction of the prior trend** after the pattern completes. They are often shorter in time than reversal patterns.

---

## Bull Flag

- **Shape:** Strong **up move** (flagpole), then a **downward-sloping** consolidation (channel or rectangle) that stays above the midpoint of the flagpole. The consolidation “flags” lower but holds structure.
- **Psychology:** Profit-taking and consolidation after a sharp rally; buyers step back in on breakout.
- **Entry:** Long on **break above** the flag’s upper boundary. **Stop:** Below the flag low (or below the flagpole’s start). **Target:** Measured move = flagpole length; project up from breakout.

```
  ═════●  Breakout
  │   ╱╲
  │  ╱  ╲   Flag
  │ ╱    ╲
  ●═══════  Flagpole
```

---

## Bear Flag

- **Shape:** Strong **down move** (flagpole), then an **upward-sloping** consolidation. Break **below** the flag’s lower boundary confirms continuation.
- **Entry:** Short on break below flag. **Stop:** Above flag high. **Target:** Flagpole length projected down.

---

## Pennant

- **Shape:** Similar to a flag but the consolidation is a **small symmetrical triangle** (converging trendlines). Forms after a sharp move (flagpole).
- **Psychology:** Brief coiling; breakout typically in the **direction of the flagpole**.
- **Entry:** In direction of prior trend on breakout of the pennant. **Stop:** Opposite side of pennant. **Target:** Flagpole length.

```
       ●  Breakout
      ╱ ╲
     ╱   ╲  Pennant
    ╱     ╲
   ●════════  Flagpole
```

---

## Ascending Triangle

- **Shape:** **Horizontal resistance** (flat top) and **rising support** (higher lows). Buyers are more aggressive; sellers at the same level. **Bullish** continuation (or reversal if after a decline).
- **Entry:** Long on **break above** resistance. **Stop:** Below the triangle (e.g. below latest low). **Target:** Height of triangle projected up from breakout.

```
  ────────────────  Resistance
   ╲    ╲    ╲
    ╲    ╲    ╲  Ascending support
     ╲    ╲
```

---

## Descending Triangle

- **Shape:** **Horizontal support** (flat bottom) and **descending resistance** (lower highs). Sellers more aggressive. **Bearish** continuation (or reversal if after a rally).
- **Entry:** Short on **break below** support. **Stop:** Above the triangle. **Target:** Height of triangle projected down.

---

## Symmetrical Triangle

- **Shape:** **Converging** support and resistance (both sloping). Neither side clearly dominant. **Breakout direction** is the signal—often in the direction of the prior trend but not always.
- **Entry:** On breakout of the triangle (above or below). **Stop:** Opposite side. **Target:** Height of triangle at widest point, projected in breakout direction.

```
      ╱╲
     ╱  ╲
    ╱    ╲
   ╱      ╲
```

---

## Rectangle (Trading Range)

- **Shape:** Price moves between **horizontal support and resistance**. Consolidation. **Breakout** (above or below) signals next move.
- **Psychology:** Balance; breakout shows which side won.
- **Entry:** On **close** beyond the rectangle. **Stop:** Inside the rectangle (other side). **Target:** Height of rectangle projected from breakout.

---

## Cup and Handle

- **Shape:** **Cup:** Rounded bottom (U-shape) after a decline. **Handle:** Small pullback (often a short flag or shallow dip) after the cup’s rim. The handle should stay in the **upper half** of the cup.
- **Psychology:** Accumulation in the cup; handle is final shakeout before breakout.
- **Entry:** Long on **break above** the handle’s resistance (rim of cup). **Stop:** Below handle low. **Target:** Depth of cup projected up from breakout.

```
       ╭──●  Breakout (rim)
      ╱    ╲
     ╱      ╲  Handle
    ╱   Cup  ╲
   ╱          ╲
  ╱            ╲
```

---

## Summary

| Pattern | Prior trend | Break | Bias |
|---------|-------------|--------|------|
| Bull Flag | Up | Above flag | Bullish |
| Bear Flag | Down | Below flag | Bearish |
| Pennant | Any (sharp) | Out of triangle | Direction of pole |
| Ascending Triangle | Often up | Above flat top | Bullish |
| Descending Triangle | Often down | Below flat bottom | Bearish |
| Symmetrical Triangle | Any | Either direction | Breakout direction |
| Rectangle | Any | Above/below range | Breakout direction |
| Cup and Handle | After decline | Above handle | Bullish |

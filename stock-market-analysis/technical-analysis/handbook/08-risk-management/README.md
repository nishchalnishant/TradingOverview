# Section 8 — Risk Management

Risk management is the **control of loss** and **sizing of exposure** so that the account can survive drawdowns and compound over time. It includes position sizing, risk per trade, risk:reward, drawdown control, and portfolio-level risk.

## Core Principles

- **Never risk more than you can afford to lose** on a single trade (e.g. 0.5–2% of account).
- **Position size** = f(account, risk%, stop distance). Same risk% whether stop is tight or wide.
- **Risk:reward:** Aim for trades where expected payoff (win rate × avg win − loss rate × avg loss) is positive. Minimum R:R often 1:1.5 or 1:2.
- **Drawdown:** Plan for **max drawdown** (e.g. 10–20%); reduce size or pause after hitting a loss limit.

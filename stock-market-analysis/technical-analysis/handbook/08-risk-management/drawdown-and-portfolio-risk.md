# Drawdown Management and Portfolio Risk

## Drawdown

- **Definition:** Peak-to-trough decline in account equity. **Max drawdown (MDD)** = largest such decline from a high to a subsequent low.
- **Why it matters:** Drawdowns are **emotional** and can trigger bad decisions (revenge trading, abandoning the plan). Planning for them (e.g. “I can tolerate 15% drawdown”) helps you **stick to the system**.
- **Rough guide:** With 1% risk per trade and ~50% win rate, expect **strings of 5–10** losses from time to time. 10 consecutive losses at 1% = ~10% drawdown. Size so that **max expected drawdown** is within your tolerance (e.g. 10–20%).

---

## Drawdown Management

- **Reduce size after drawdown:** Some traders **halve** risk% after e.g. 10% drawdown until equity makes a new high (“recovery mode”). Reduces chance of a second drawdown extending the first.
- **Pause after N losses:** Stop trading after e.g. 3–5 consecutive losses (or after losing X% in a day) to avoid emotional trading. Resume when calm and when setup quality is restored.
- **Never “double up”** to recover a loss (e.g. risk 2% on the next trade to “get back”). That increases risk of ruin.

---

## Portfolio Risk

- **Multiple positions:** If you have 5 open trades, each risking 1%, **theoretical** max loss = 5% if all stop out. In practice, not all will stop the same day—but **correlation** matters. Long SPY + long QQQ = highly correlated; two losses can come together.
- **Correlation risk:** Diversify across **uncorrelated** instruments or strategies so that one bad day doesn’t hit every position. Avoid over-concentration in one sector or one theme.
- **Leverage:** Leverage **amplifies** both gains and drawdowns. Keep leverage moderate (e.g. 1–2× notional for beginners) so that a string of losses doesn’t blow the account.

---

## Compounding Strategies

- **Reinvest profits (compound):** As account grows, **same risk%** means **larger dollar** risk and larger position size. Growth accelerates but drawdowns in dollar terms get bigger. Ensure you’re comfortable with that.
- **Fixed dollar risk:** Alternatively, keep **dollar** risk constant (e.g. always $500 per trade) so that drawdowns in dollar terms are stable as account grows. Growth is linear rather than compound but psychology may be easier.
- **Withdrawals:** If you withdraw profits regularly, effective “account” for sizing is the remaining capital. Size based on **current** equity, not peak.

---

## Formulas and Examples

- **Max drawdown (from risk and win rate):** No exact formula; use **simulation** or historical backtest. Rule of thumb: with 1% risk and 50% win rate, expect drawdowns of **10–20%** over a year in a typical strategy.
- **Recovery:** After a 20% drawdown, you need **25%** return to get back to even (0.80 × 1.25 = 1.0). So avoiding large drawdowns is key to long-term compounding.

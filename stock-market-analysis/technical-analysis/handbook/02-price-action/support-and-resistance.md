# Support and Resistance

## Concept

- **Support:** A price level where **buying interest** has historically appeared. Price tends to bounce or consolidate there. Often at prior lows, round numbers, or volume nodes.
- **Resistance:** A price level where **selling interest** has historically appeared. Price tends to reject or stall. Often at prior highs, round numbers, or volume nodes.

```
        Resistance ─────●─────●─────  (sellers step in)
                        │     │
    Price               │     │
                        │     │
        Support   ─────●─────●─────  (buyers step in)
```

---

## Why Levels Work

- **Memory:** Participants remember where price reversed or paused; they place orders there again.
- **Unfilled orders:** Limit orders left at prior highs/lows get filled when price returns.
- **Psychology:** Round numbers (100, 50.00) and obvious prior swing points attract attention and orders.

---

## Chart Interpretation

- **Multiple touches:** The more times a level is tested and holds, the more “valid” it is—but also the more likely it is to eventually break (liquidity exhausted).
- **Strength:** A level that caused a **sharp reversal** is often stronger than one that only caused a small pause.
- **Role reversal:** After a **breakout**, former resistance can act as **support** (and vice versa)—“resistance becomes support.”

---

## Trading Psychology

- **Blind faith:** Don’t assume every touch will hold; use **confirmation** (e.g. bullish/bearish candle, volume) and always use a stop.
- **Breakouts:** Failed breakouts (fakeouts) are common; wait for **close beyond** the level or a retest before adding.

---

## Entry/Exit Strategy

- **Long at support:** Enter on a bounce (e.g. bullish candle or engulfing) with stop below the level. Target next resistance or measured move.
- **Short at resistance:** Enter on a rejection with stop above the level. Target next support.
- **Breakout:** Enter on a close beyond the level (or pullback to it); stop on the other side of the level.

---

## Risk Management

- **Stop placement:** Place stop **beyond** the level (below support for longs, above resistance for shorts) so that a clean break invalidates the trade.
- **Position size:** Size so that (entry − stop) × size = your fixed risk per trade (e.g. 1% of account).

---

## Example Setup

- **Setup:** Price makes three touches of support at 98.50 and holds. Fourth touch shows a hammer and volume increase.
- **Entry:** Long on break of hammer high or next candle open. Stop below 98.50 (e.g. 98.20). Target prior swing high or 1:2 R:R.

# Section 2 — Price Action

Price action is the study of **raw price movement** (and often volume) without relying solely on indicators. It focuses on structure: support/resistance, trendlines, break of structure, and how markets transition between range and trend.

## Key Concepts

- **Support:** Level where buying interest tends to appear; price bounces or holds.
- **Resistance:** Level where selling interest tends to appear; price rejects or stalls.
- **Higher High (HH) / Higher Low (HL):** Bullish structure in an uptrend.
- **Lower High (LH) / Lower Low (LL):** Bearish structure in a downtrend.
- **Break of structure (BOS):** Price breaks a prior HH or LL—can signal trend continuation or reversal depending on context.
- **Market structure shift:** Prior structure (e.g. series of HLs) is broken; often used as a reversal or change-of-trend signal.

---

## Trading Psychology

- **Levels:** Don’t assume every touch of support/resistance will hold; use **confirmation** (e.g. candle close, volume) and **stops**.
- **Structure:** Accept that structure is defined in hindsight; trade **inválidation** (e.g. stop below last HL in an uptrend) rather than prediction.

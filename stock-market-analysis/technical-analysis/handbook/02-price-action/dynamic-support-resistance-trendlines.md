# Dynamic Support/Resistance and Trendlines

## Concept

**Dynamic** levels move with price (or time), unlike fixed horizontal support/resistance. They include **moving averages**, **trendlines**, and **channels**. They define the current trend and often act as support in uptrends and resistance in downtrends.

---

## Trendlines

- **Uptrend line:** Drawn along **successive higher lows** (touch at least two lows; more touches = stronger).
- **Downtrend line:** Drawn along **successive lower highs**.

```
Uptrend line:                    Downtrend line:
    ●                                  ●     ●
     \                                  \   /
      \   ●                               \ /
       \ /                                  ●
        ●
   (connect the lows)              (connect the highs)
```

- **Use:** Entries on pullbacks to the trendline; exit or reduce when price breaks the line (especially on a close).

---

## Channels

- **Channel:** Two parallel lines—one along swing highs, one along swing lows. Price often oscillates within the channel.
- **Trading:** Long near lower boundary, short near upper; or trade breakouts of the channel with confirmation.

```
    ───────────────────  Upper channel line
       ●     ●     ●
      / \   / \   /
     /   \ /   \ /
    ●     ●     ●
    ───────────────────  Lower channel line
```

---

## Dynamic Levels (e.g. Moving Averages)

- **EMA 20, 50, 200:** Often act as support in uptrends and resistance in downtrends. Price “respecting” the MA suggests trend strength.
- **VWAP:** Intraday dynamic level; price above VWAP = bullish bias; below = bearish. Often used for mean reversion or trend confirmation.

---

## Chart Interpretation

- **Valid trendline:** At least two touches; not over-curved (not connecting every wick). Break of trendline can signal **weakening trend** or **reversal**.
- **Channel:** Break above upper line (in uptrend) can mean acceleration; break below lower line can mean trend change.

---

## Trading Psychology

- **Redrawing:** Avoid constantly redrawing trendlines to fit every wiggle; use **clear swing points** and accept that some breaks will be false.
- **MAs:** Don’t treat MAs as magic; they are **smoothed price**—useful in trending markets, noisy in choppy markets.

---

## Entry/Exit and Risk

- **Entry:** Long on touch of uptrend line or EMA with bullish confirmation; stop below the line or recent low.
- **Exit:** Trail stop along the trendline or below recent swing lows; reduce or exit on break of structure.
- **Risk:** In strong trends, dynamic levels hold repeatedly; in weak trends, they break—always use a stop.

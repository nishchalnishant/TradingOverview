# Market Structure Shift and Liquidity Grabs

## Concept

A **market structure shift (MSS)** is when the prior trend structure is **broken** in a way that suggests a **change of character**—e.g. in an uptrend, price makes a **Lower Low (LL)** instead of a Higher Low, or breaks below a key HL. This can signal a **reversal** or a **deeper pullback**. **Liquidity grabs** and **stop hunts** are short moves that sweep obvious stops (e.g. below a low or above a high) and then reverse, often leaving a wick.

---

## Market Structure Shift (MSS)

- **In an uptrend:** First **Lower Low** (breaking the sequence of HLs) = bearish structure shift. Suggests buyers failed to hold structure; possible reversal or correction.
- **In a downtrend:** First **Higher High** (breaking the sequence of LHs) = bullish structure shift. Suggests sellers lost control; possible reversal or bounce.

```
Uptrend then MSS:
      ●  HH
     / \
    /   ●  HL
   /     \
  ●       ●  ← LL (breaks HL sequence) = MSS bearish
```

- **Use:** MSS is often used as a **condition** for reversal entries (e.g. look for short after bearish MSS, with confirmation).

---

## Liquidity Grabs / Stop Hunts

- **Idea:** Price briefly moves beyond a visible level (e.g. swing low, round number) where many stops sit, triggers those stops, then reverses. On the chart this often shows as a **wick** (e.g. long lower wick below support).
- **Why:** Market makers or large players can profit from running stops; or it’s simply the result of order flow (stops getting hit, then buying).
- **Implication:** Don’t place stops **exactly** at the obvious level; place them slightly beyond, or use a **close** beyond the level as invalidation.

---

## Chart Interpretation

- **MSS:** Wait for a **clear** break of structure (e.g. close below last HL in uptrend), not just a wick.
- **Liquidity grab:** Long wick below support with a strong close back inside range = potential reversal; entry on confirmation (e.g. next candle) with stop below the wick low.

---

## Trading Psychology

- **Stop hunts:** Expect them in volatile markets; avoid placing stops at the **exact** round number or obvious low/high. Use buffer or close-based invalidation.
- **MSS:** Don’t assume every MSS leads to a full reversal; it can be a deeper pullback in a trend. Use **targets** and **trail stops**.

---

## Entry/Exit and Risk

- **After bearish MSS:** Look for short on retest of the broken structure (e.g. prior HL now acting as resistance), with stop above the MSS high. Target next support or LL.
- **After liquidity grab:** Long on confirmation (e.g. bullish close or engulfing) with stop below the wick low. Target prior resistance or measured move.
- **Risk:** Always use a stop; MSS and liquidity grabs can fail (e.g. continuation of the original trend).

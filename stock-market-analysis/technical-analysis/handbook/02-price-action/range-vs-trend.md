# Range vs Trend: Consolidation and Expansion

## Concept

Markets alternate between **expansion** (trending, directional moves) and **contraction** (range, consolidation). In **range** markets, price oscillates between support and resistance. In **trend** markets, price makes sustained HH/HL or LH/LL. Strategies that work in one often fail in the other.

---

## Range (Consolidation)

- **Characteristics:** Price bounces between roughly horizontal support and resistance; no clear sequence of HH/HL or LH/LL. Often lower volume and volatility.
- **Psychology:** Buyers and sellers are in balance; no side has control. Can precede a **breakout** (expansion) in either direction.

```
    Resistance ─────────────●────●────●────
                            │    │    │
    Price                   │    │    │
                            │    │    │
    Support     ────────────●────●────●────
```

- **Trading:** Fade the range (buy near support, sell near resistance) with tight stops; or wait for **breakout** and trade the expansion.

---

## Trend (Expansion)

- **Characteristics:** Clear HH/HL (uptrend) or LH/LL (downtrend); often higher volume on trend moves; pullbacks are shallower and hold structure.
- **Psychology:** One side has control; participants add in the direction of the trend. “The trend is your friend.”

- **Trading:** Trade in the direction of the trend (pullbacks to structure, or break of structure); avoid counter-trend unless you have a clear reversal setup.

---

## Consolidation vs Expansion

| | Range (Consolidation) | Trend (Expansion) |
|---|------------------------|-------------------|
| **Structure** | No clear HH/HL or LH/LL | Clear HH/HL or LH/LL |
| **Strategy** | Fade extremes or wait for breakout | Follow trend, buy dips / sell rallies |
| **Indicators** | Oscillators (RSI, Stochastic) useful for overbought/oversold | MAs, trendlines, structure |
| **Risk** | Breakout can stop out fades | False breakouts, late entries |

---

## Chart Interpretation

- **Before trading:** Identify whether you’re in a **range** or **trend** on your timeframe. Use higher timeframe for bias, lower for entry.
- **Breakout:** First move out of a range is often **tested** (retest of the broken level); wait for confirmation or enter on retest.

---

## Trading Psychology

- **Range:** Avoid chasing breakouts until you see **confirmation** (e.g. close beyond level, volume); many breakouts fail (false breakouts).
- **Trend:** Avoid fighting the trend; counter-trend trades need a clear setup (e.g. MSS, exhaustion, key level).

---

## Risk Management

- **Range:** Stops should be **beyond** the support/resistance; expect whipsaws near the boundaries.
- **Trend:** Stops below structure (e.g. below HL in uptrend); avoid stops that are too tight (normal pullbacks can hit them).

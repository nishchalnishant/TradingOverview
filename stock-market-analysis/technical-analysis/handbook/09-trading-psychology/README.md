# Section 9 — Trading Psychology

Trading psychology is the **mental and emotional** side of trading: discipline, fear and greed, biases, journaling, and having a clear trading plan. Many traders fail because of **psychological** errors rather than lack of strategy.

## Key Ideas

- **Discipline:** Following your **rules** (entry, exit, stop, size) even when emotions push you to break them.
- **Fear and greed:** Fear causes early exits or avoiding valid setups; greed causes overtrading, overleveraging, or holding losers. Awareness and rules help.
- **Revenge trading:** Trading to “get back” a loss—usually leads to larger losses. **Overtrading:** Taking too many trades (noise, FOMO)—erodes edge and increases costs.
- **Journal:** Record setups, outcomes, and **emotional state** to find patterns (e.g. “I lose when I trade after a loss”).
- **Plan:** Written **trading plan** (when to trade, what to trade, risk, exit rules) reduces ad-hoc decisions and emotional trading.

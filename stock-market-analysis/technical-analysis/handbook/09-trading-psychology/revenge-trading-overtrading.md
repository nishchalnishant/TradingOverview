# Revenge Trading and Overtrading

## Revenge Trading

- **Definition:** Trading **immediately** after a loss (or string of losses) with the **goal of getting the money back**—often with larger size or looser rules. Emotion-driven, not plan-driven.
- **Why it’s harmful:** You’re not waiting for a **valid setup**; you’re forcing a trade. That increases the chance of another loss and can lead to a **runaway** drawdown.
- **How to avoid:** (1) **Rule:** No new trade for X minutes (e.g. 30–60) after a loss. (2) **Max daily loss:** Stop trading after losing e.g. 2% in a day. (3) **Awareness:** If you catch yourself thinking “I need to get that back,” **step away**—close the platform, take a break. (4) **Journal:** Note “revenge” in the journal when it happens; patterns will show up.

---

## Overtrading

- **Definition:** Taking **too many** trades: trades that don’t meet your setup criteria, or trading in conditions (e.g. low volatility, no clear level) where your edge doesn’t apply. Often driven by **boredom** or **FOMO**.
- **Why it’s harmful:** Each trade has **cost** (spread, commission) and **risk**. Overtrading increases costs and often lowers win rate (low-quality setups). Can also lead to **mental fatigue** and more mistakes.
- **How to avoid:** (1) **Strict setup criteria:** Only trade when **all** conditions are met (level, confirmation, timeframe). (2) **Max trades per day:** Cap at e.g. 3–5 so you’re forced to be selective. (3) **“No setup” is a valid outcome:** It’s okay to have days with zero trades. (4) **Journal:** Count how many trades were “A+ setup” vs “meh”; reduce the “meh” ones.

---

## Trading Psychology

- **Boredom:** If you trade to “have something to do,” you’ll overtrade. Find other activities; treat trading as **business**, not entertainment.
- **FOMO:** Missing one move feels bad, but **chasing** usually feels worse (bad entry, quick stop). Remind yourself: there will be other setups. Your edge requires **patience**.

---

## Risk Management

- **Revenge:** One revenge trade can risk 2× or 3× your normal size—so **one** revenge trade can equal several normal losses. Rules (pause after loss, max daily loss) are **risk management**.
- **Overtrading:** More trades = more exposure. On bad days, many small losses add up. **Quality over quantity.**

# Trading Journaling and Performance Analysis

## Why Journal?

- **Objectivity:** Memory is biased (we remember wins and forget losses). A **written** record shows what actually happened.
- **Improvement:** You can see **patterns**: e.g. “I lose when I trade in the first 15 minutes” or “My win rate on pullback entries is 60% but on breakout entries 40%.” That guides what to do more of and what to avoid.
- **Accountability:** Writing “I broke my rule because…” forces honesty and reduces repeated mistakes.

---

## What to Record

- **Setup:** Instrument, timeframe, type of setup (e.g. pullback to EMA, breakout), key level.
- **Entry:** Price, time, why you entered (e.g. “bullish engulfing at support”).
- **Exit:** Price, time, reason (stop, target, manual). **P&L** (in $ and in R if you use R-multiples).
- **Emotional state:** Calm, anxious, revenge, FOMO, etc. (optional but valuable).
- **Rule adherence:** Did you follow your plan? If not, what did you do and why?
- **Screenshot (optional):** Chart at entry; helps review later.

---

## Performance Metrics

- **Win rate:** % of trades that are profitable. Not sufficient alone (you can have 90% win rate and lose money if the 10% losses are huge).
- **Average R (or avg win vs avg loss):** Average winner in “R” (multiples of risk). e.g. If you risk $100 per trade, and average winner is $200, that’s 2R. **Expectancy** = (Win rate × Avg win in R) − (Loss rate × 1) = avg R per trade. Positive = edge.
- **Max drawdown:** Largest peak-to-trough decline. Track to see if you’re within tolerance.
- **Profit factor:** Gross profit / Gross loss. > 1 = profitable.
- **By setup type:** Break down by strategy (e.g. pullback vs breakout) or by instrument to see what works best for you.

---

## Review cadence

- **Per trade:** Log right after the trade (or end of day).
- **Weekly:** Quick review—win rate, avg R, any rule breaks. Adjust next week (e.g. “fewer breakout trades, more pullbacks”).
- **Monthly:** Deeper review—expectancy, drawdown, best/worst trades. Refine plan if needed.

---

## Trading Psychology

- **No blame, no excuse:** Journal is for **learning**, not for justifying. “Market was manipulated” doesn’t help; “I placed stop at obvious low—next time I’ll use a buffer” does.
- **Patterns:** If you see “I revenge trade after 2 losses,” create a **rule** (e.g. after 2 losses, no trade for 1 hour) and log whether you followed it.

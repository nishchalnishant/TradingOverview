# Emotional Discipline and Fear/Greed Cycles

## Concept

**Emotional discipline** means making trading decisions based on your **plan and rules**, not on short-term emotions. **Fear** and **greed** are the two main drivers of bad decisions: fear leads to closing winners early or avoiding valid setups; greed leads to overtrading, overleveraging, or holding losers too long.

---

## Fear

- **Manifestations:** Exiting a winning trade too early (“take profit before it reverses”). Not taking a valid setup (“what if it’s wrong?”). Moving stop to breakeven too soon (then getting stopped out on noise). Avoiding trading after a loss.
- **Impact:** Reduces **average winner** and **number of trades**; strategy can’t perform as designed.
- **Mitigation:** Write **exit rules** (target, trailing stop) and **don’t** close early unless the rule says so. Use **position size** so that one loss is acceptable (reduces fear of loss). After a loss, **next trade** is independent—stick to the plan.

---

## Greed

- **Manifestations:** Adding to a winning position without a plan (“it’s going to the moon”). Taking trades that don’t meet your criteria (FOMO). Using excessive leverage. Holding a loser hoping it comes back (“it will reverse”).
- **Impact:** One or two bad trades can wipe out many good ones; drawdowns and blow-ups.
- **Mitigation:** **Pre-define** add rules (e.g. add only on pullback to structure) or don’t add at all. **No trade** without a valid setup. **Stop loss** is non-negotiable—no “I’ll give it more room.”

---

## Fear and Greed Cycles

- Markets often form **tops** when greed is high (everyone is bullish, FOMO) and **bottoms** when fear is high (capitulation, “never trading again”). Contrarian sentiment is a rough guide but not a precise timing tool.
- **As a trader:** When you feel **very confident** (greed), tighten risk (e.g. take partial profit). When you feel **very scared** (fear), don’t change the plan—stick to size and stops; avoid revenge or doubling down.

---

## Building Discipline

- **Rules in writing:** Write entry, exit, stop, and size rules. When in doubt, **refer to the plan** instead of “feeling.”
- **Routine:** Same pre-market routine (review levels, check bias). Same post-trade routine (journal, no immediate next trade). Reduces impulsive decisions.
- **Acceptance:** Losses are part of trading. Accept that you will have losing trades and drawdowns; the edge plays out over **many** trades.

---

## Trading Psychology

- **Self-awareness:** Notice when you’re **afraid** (e.g. closing early) or **greedy** (e.g. adding size). Pause and ask: “Is this my plan or my emotion?”
- **No perfection:** Discipline doesn’t mean never slipping. It means **catching** yourself and returning to the plan.

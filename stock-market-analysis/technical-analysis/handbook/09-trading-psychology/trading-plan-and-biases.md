# Building a Trading Plan and Cognitive Biases

## Trading Plan

A **trading plan** is a **written** set of rules that define when and how you trade. It reduces **discretion** and **emotional** decisions.

**Include:**

1. **Markets and timeframes:** What you trade (e.g. indices, forex) and on what timeframe (e.g. 4H for structure, 1H for entry).
2. **Setup criteria:** Exact conditions for entry (e.g. “pullback to 20 EMA in an HH/HL uptrend; bullish engulfing; enter on break of engulfing high”).
3. **Risk:** Risk per trade (e.g. 1%); max open positions; max daily loss (e.g. stop after 2% daily loss).
4. **Entry:** Order type (limit vs market), where (e.g. at level, on break).
5. **Exit:** Stop placement (e.g. below HL); target (e.g. 1:2 R:R or next resistance); trailing rules if any.
6. **When NOT to trade:** e.g. First 15 min after open; before major news; after 3 consecutive losses; when sick or emotionally off.
7. **Review:** How often you review the journal and the plan (e.g. weekly); when you’ll revise the plan (e.g. quarterly, or after a large drawdown with cause identified).

## Cognitive Biases in Trading

- **Confirmation bias:** Seeking information that supports your view (e.g. only reading bullish news when long). **Mitigation:** Actively look for **opposite** views; define **invalidation** before the trade.
- **Recency bias:** Overweighting recent events (e.g. “last 3 trades were winners, I’m on a roll”). **Mitigation:** Each trade is independent; stick to same risk and rules.
- **Anchoring:** Fixating on a price (e.g. “I’ll sell when it gets back to my entry”). **Mitigation:** Use **structure** (support, resistance) and **plan** (target, stop), not arbitrary numbers.
- **Loss aversion:** Feeling losses more than gains; leading to holding losers (hope) or cutting winners early (fear). **Mitigation:** Define stop and target **before** entry; treat each trade as a probability, not “my money.”
- **Overconfidence:** After a winning streak, increasing size or taking lower-quality setups. **Mitigation:** Fixed risk%; same setup criteria always.
- **FOMO:** Fear of missing out—entering late, chasing. **Mitigation:** “No trade” is acceptable; wait for the next setup.

---

## Trading Psychology

- **Plan as contract:** The plan is a **contract with yourself**. When you’re tempted to break it, ask: “Would I have written this exception when I was calm?” If not, don’t do it.
- **Revise deliberately:** Change the plan only after **review** (e.g. monthly), with **data** (journal, backtest), not in the heat of the moment.

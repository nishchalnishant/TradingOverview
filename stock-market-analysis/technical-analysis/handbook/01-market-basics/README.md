## Key Takeaways

- **Markets** are venues where buyers and sellers agree on price (price discovery).
- **Participants** range from retail traders to central banks; each affects liquidity and volatility.
- **Order types** (market vs limit vs stop) define when and at what price your order fills.
- **Bid/ask and spread** determine execution cost and slippage.
- **Sessions** (Asian, European, US) create distinct volatility and liquidity patterns.

---

## Trading Psychology Implications

- Understanding **who is on the other side** of your trade reduces fear of “manipulation.”
- Knowing **when** (which session) you trade helps set realistic expectations for volatility and spread.
- Using the **right order type** (e.g., limits in thin markets) reduces regret and slippage.

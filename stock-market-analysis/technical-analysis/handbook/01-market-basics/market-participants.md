# Market Participants

## Who Is in the Market?

Markets are a mix of **retail, institutional, and intermediary** participants. Each group has different size, speed, and motives—which shapes order flow and volatility.

```
  RETAIL TRADERS  —— small size, emotional, discretionary
         ↓
  BROKERS / ECNs  —— route orders to venues
         ↓
  MARKET MAKERS   —— provide liquidity, capture spread
         ↓
  INSTITUTIONS    —— funds, banks, corporates; large size
         ↓
  HFT / ALGOS     —— speed, arbitrage, market making
```

---

## Retail Traders

- **Who:** Individual traders and small accounts.
- **Behavior:** Often reactive (news, social media); prone to FOMO and panic.
- **Impact:** In aggregate, retail can move prices in highly retail-driven names (meme stocks, small caps).

---

## Institutional Investors

- **Who:** Mutual funds, hedge funds, pension funds, insurers.
- **Behavior:** Fundamental and systematic; large orders executed over time (TWAP, VWAP, algos).
- **Impact:** Their flow creates **support/resistance** (e.g. where they accumulated) and **trends** when they rebalance or deploy capital.

---

## Market Makers

- **Who:** Firms that quote bid and ask and provide liquidity.
- **Behavior:** Profit from spread and rebates; manage inventory by adjusting quotes.
- **Impact:** Tighten spread in normal conditions; may widen or step back in stress.

---

## High-Frequency Trading (HFT)

- **Who:** Algo firms trading on speed and microstructure.
- **Behavior:** Arbitrage, market making, order flow prediction.
- **Impact:** Tighter spreads and more liquidity in liquid names; in stress, liquidity can vanish quickly.

---

## Central Banks and Corporates

- **Who:** Central banks (FX, rates), corporates (hedging, treasury).
- **Behavior:** Macro and hedging; often large, less price-sensitive orders.
- **Impact:** Can cause sustained moves (e.g. rate decisions, intervention).

---

## Trading Psychology

- **Retail vs smart money:** Don’t assume you’re always wrong; focus on **structure and volume** to see who is in control.
- **Institutional footprints:** Large volume at key levels (breakouts, VWAP) often reflects institutional interest.

---

## Risk Management

- **Liquidity:** In names dominated by retail or low volume, execution can be worse; size down or use limits.
- **News:** Around macro or earnings, institutional and algo flow spikes; consider reducing size or waiting for the first reaction.

# Types of Markets

## Overview

Different asset classes have different **trading hours, leverage, liquidity, and drivers**. Choosing the right market for your strategy and risk tolerance is part of edge.

| Market | Examples | Hours | Leverage | Key Drivers |
|--------|----------|--------|----------|-------------|
| **Equity** | Stocks, ETFs | Exchange hours | Varies (margin) | Earnings, macro, sector rotation |
| **Forex** | EUR/USD, GBP/JPY | 24/5 | High (e.g. 50:1) | Central banks, rates, geopolitics |
| **Crypto** | BTC, ETH | 24/7 | Very high | Sentiment, regulation, flows |
| **Commodities** | Gold, Oil, Corn | Session + electronic | Moderate–high | Supply/demand, USD, real rates |

---

## Equity Markets

- **What:** Shares of companies; listed on exchanges (e.g. NYSE, NASDAQ).
- **Liquidity:** Usually high in large caps; low in small caps and pre/post market.
- **Psychology:** Earnings and news cause gaps; trend and mean reversion both work depending on timeframe.

---

## Forex (FX)

- **What:** Pairs of currencies (e.g. EUR/USD = euros per dollar).
- **Liquidity:** Highest in major pairs during London and NY overlap.
- **Psychology:** Session overlaps (London/NY) see most volatility; weekends and holidays thin liquidity.

---

## Crypto

- **What:** Digital assets on various venues; spot and derivatives.
- **Liquidity:** Varies by exchange and pair; can be thin and prone to wicks.
- **Psychology:** 24/7 trading increases FOMO and revenge trading; strict risk rules are essential.

---

## Commodities

- **What:** Physical or derivative exposure to metals, energy, agriculture.
- **Liquidity:** Good in front-month futures and major ETFs.
- **Psychology:** Macro and seasonality matter; contango/backwardation affect roll cost.

---

## Chart Interpretation

- **Equity:** Watch for gaps at open, VWAP, and session highs/lows.
- **Forex:** Mark Asian range, London open, NY open; trade breakouts or fades of those levels.
- **Crypto:** Expect large wicks and overnight moves; use wider stops or lower size.
- **Commodities:** Align with macro (e.g. gold vs real yields) and contract roll dates.

---

## Risk Management

- **Leverage:** Forex and crypto often offer high leverage; keep position size small per trade.
- **Slippage:** Crypto and illiquid names can slip; use limits and avoid market orders in thin conditions.
- **Correlation:** Don’t treat “different” markets as uncorrelated; risk can compound (e.g. risk-off across stocks and crypto).

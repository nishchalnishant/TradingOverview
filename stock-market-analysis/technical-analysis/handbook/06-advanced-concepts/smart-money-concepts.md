# Smart Money Concepts (SMC)

## Concept

**Smart Money Concepts (SMC)** is a way of reading the market from the perspective of **institutional** or “smart” money: where they might have entered (order blocks), where retail stops sit (liquidity), and where price is “unbalanced” (fair value gaps). The idea is to align with institutional flow rather than be on the wrong side of it.

---

## Order Blocks

- **Definition:** The **last opposing candle** (or small cluster of candles) before a **strong move** in one direction. That candle’s range is the “order block”—a zone where large orders may have been placed before the move.
- **Bullish order block:** Last bearish candle(s) before a strong **up** move. The low/high of that candle can act as **support** on retests.
- **Bearish order block:** Last bullish candle(s) before a strong **down** move. The high/low of that candle can act as **resistance** on retests.

```
  Strong move up
  ═════●
  │   ███  ← Last bearish candle = Bullish OB (support on retest)
  ●   ███
```

- **Trading:** Look for **retests** of order blocks in the direction of the prior move. Entry on confirmation (e.g. bullish candle at bullish OB). Stop below/above the order block.
- **Context:** Stronger when it aligns with **structure** (e.g. OB at a HL in an uptrend) and **liquidity** (e.g. after a sweep of lows).

---

## Liquidity Zones

- **Definition:** Areas where **stops** are likely clustered—e.g. **above** recent swing highs (buy stops for shorts) and **below** recent swing lows (sell stops for longs). Price often **sweeps** these levels (liquidity grab) then reverses.
- **Equal highs/lows:** Multiple touches of the same high or low create “liquidity pools.” A break that **fails** (sweep then reverse) is a classic **liquidity grab** and can set up a reversal (e.g. long after sweep of equal lows).
- **Trading:** Don’t place your stop **exactly** at the obvious high/low; use a buffer or wait for **close** beyond the level. Use sweeps as potential **entry** zones (e.g. long after sweep of lows with confirmation).

---

## Fair Value Gap (FVG) / Market Imbalance

- **Definition:** A **three-candle** pattern where the **middle** candle’s range does **not overlap** with the first candle’s range. This leaves a “gap” or **imbalance**—price moved so fast that there was no trading in that zone.
- **Bullish FVG:** Gap **up** between candle 1 high and candle 3 low. Price often **returns** to “fill” the gap (fill = price trades through the gap zone).
- **Bearish FVG:** Gap **down** between candle 1 low and candle 3 high. Often filled on a pullback.

```
  Candle 3   ────●
                │  ← Gap (no overlap with candle 1)
  Candle 2   ────●
  Candle 1   ────●
```

- **Trading:** **Entry:** In trend, enter in the direction of the FVG when price returns to fill it (e.g. long when price pulls back into a bullish FVG in an uptrend). **Target:** Opposite side of FVG or next structure. **Stop:** Beyond the FVG or recent structure.
- **Institutional interpretation:** FVG can represent **institutional imbalance**—one-sided flow that may get filled later as price mean-reverts into the gap.

---

## Break of Structure (BOS) and Change of Character (CHoCH)

- **BOS:** Price breaks a prior HH or LL (continuation of trend or start of new trend). See [02 — Trend and Structure](../02-price-action/trend-and-structure.md).
- **CHoCH (Change of Character):** When the **type** of structure changes—e.g. in an uptrend, price makes a **Lower Low** (breaks the sequence of HLs). This “change of character” can signal a potential **reversal** or deeper correction. Used in SMC as a **condition** for reversal entries (e.g. wait for CHoCH before looking for short at order block).

---

## Trading Psychology

- **Don’t fight the flow:** SMC emphasizes trading **with** where institutions are likely positioned (order blocks, after liquidity sweeps) rather than fading every move.
- **Stops:** Understanding liquidity zones helps you **avoid** placing stops where the market is likely to sweep (e.g. just below a visible low).

---

## Risk Management

- **Order blocks:** Stop beyond the order block (below bullish OB for longs). One failed OB touch doesn’t mean the level is dead—but respect invalidation.
- **FVG:** If price **fully fills** the FVG and breaks through, the trade is invalid; exit or tighten stop.
- **Liquidity sweeps:** Enter only after **confirmation** (e.g. strong candle or close back inside range); avoid chasing the sweep.

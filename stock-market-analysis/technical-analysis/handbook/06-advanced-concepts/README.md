# Section 6 — Advanced Trading Concepts

Advanced concepts focus on **institutional** and **order-flow** thinking: where big players might be positioned (order blocks, liquidity zones), how markets can be manipulated (stop hunts, fair value gaps), and structured frameworks like **Wyckoff** for accumulation and distribution.

## Key Ideas

- **Order blocks:** Last opposing candle before a strong move; potential support/resistance where institutions may have entered.
- **Liquidity zones:** Areas where stops cluster (above highs, below lows); price often sweeps them before reversing (liquidity grab).
- **Fair Value Gap (FVG):** Imbalance—three candles where the middle candle’s range doesn’t overlap with the first or third; often “filled” later.
- **Wyckoff:** Phases of accumulation (markup preparation) and distribution (markdown preparation); springs (false breakdown) and upthrusts (false breakout) as traps.

---

## Trading Psychology

- These concepts explain **why** price might reverse at a level (order block, FVG fill) or **sweep** a level (liquidity grab). Use them to **place stops** (beyond obvious liquidity) and **entries** (at order blocks or after a sweep).
- Avoid treating every move as “manipulation”; many moves are normal **order flow** and **liquidity** dynamics.

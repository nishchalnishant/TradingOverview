# Wyckoff Method

## Concept

The **Wyckoff Method** is a framework for understanding **accumulation** (smart money buying before markup) and **distribution** (smart money selling before markdown). It uses **price and volume** to identify phases and specific events (e.g. **springs**, **upthrusts**) that often precede the next big move.

---

## Accumulation Phases (Before Markup)

After a downtrend, price may enter an **accumulation** phase: institutions are buying while retail is selling. Key elements:

1. **Selling climax (SC):** High volume sell-off; possible exhaustion.
2. **Secondary test (ST):** Price returns to the low area on lower volume; holds (doesn’t make a much lower low).
3. **Spring:** Price **breaks below** the range low (below support), then **reverses** quickly back into the range. A **false breakdown**—stops are run, then buyers step in. Often the last shakeout before markup.
4. **Sign of strength (SOS):** Strong up move with volume, breaking above the range.
5. **Last point of support (LPS):** Pullback to the range (or broken resistance now support) before the **markup** (sustained uptrend).

```
    Range
  ─────●─────
  │         │
  │  Spring │  (break below, then reverse)
  ●─────────●
       ST
```

---

## Distribution Phases (Before Markdown)

After an uptrend, price may enter **distribution**: institutions are selling while retail is buying.

1. **Buying climax (BC):** High volume rally; possible exhaustion.
2. **Secondary test:** Price returns to the high area on lower volume; fails to make a much higher high.
3. **Upthrust:** Price **breaks above** the range high, then **reverses** back into the range. A **false breakout**—stops run, then sellers step in. Often the last trap before markdown.
4. **Sign of weakness (SOW):** Strong down move with volume.
5. **Last point of supply (LPSY):** Bounce to the range (or broken support now resistance) before **markdown** (sustained downtrend).

---

## Springs and Upthrusts

- **Spring:** False **breakdown** below support. Price dips below the range, triggers stops, then **reverses** and closes back inside (or above) the range. **Bullish** signal when it occurs in an accumulation context (volume, prior structure).
- **Upthrust:** False **breakout** above resistance. Price spikes above the range, triggers stops, then **reverses** back inside (or below) the range. **Bearish** signal in a distribution context.

**Trading:** Enter in the **direction of the reversal** after the spring (long) or upthrust (short), with stop beyond the spring/upthrust extreme. Confirm with volume and next candle.

---

## Wyckoff and Volume

- **Volume** is central: climaxes (SC, BC) often have high volume; secondary tests and springs/upthrusts can show **lower** volume on the false move and **higher** volume on the reversal.
- **Volume confirms** whether a break is likely to follow through (high volume) or fail (low volume break, then reversal on high volume).

---

## Trading Psychology

- **Patience:** Wyckoff phases can take time. Don’t force entries; wait for **spring/upthrust** or **SOS/SOW** with confirmation.
- **Traps:** Springs and upthrusts are **traps** for retail (stops run). Use them as **setup** zones (e.g. long after a spring) rather than getting stopped out by placing stops exactly at the range boundary.

---

## Risk Management

- **Spring long:** Stop below the **low** of the spring (or below the range). Target = range height or next resistance.
- **Upthrust short:** Stop above the **high** of the upthrust (or above the range). Target = range height or next support.
- **Invalidation:** If after a spring price breaks below the spring low and holds, the accumulation thesis is damaged; exit.

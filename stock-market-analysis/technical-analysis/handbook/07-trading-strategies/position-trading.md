# Position Trading Strategies

## Concept

**Position trading** holds positions for **weeks to months**, aligning with **major trend** and often **macro** or **fundamental** themes. Fewer trades; larger moves per trade. Uses **higher timeframes** (weekly, daily) and **trend structure**; less focus on short-term noise.

---

## Setup Conditions

- **Timeframe:** **Weekly** and **daily** for trend and structure. Entry can be refined on **daily** or **4H** (e.g. pullback to weekly support).
- **Trend:** Clear **higher highs and higher lows** (uptrend) or **lower highs and lower lows** (downtrend) on weekly/daily. Optional: alignment with **macro** (e.g. rates, growth) or **sector** strength.
- **Levels:** Major support/resistance (weekly swing points, round numbers, key MAs like 200 EMA). Position trades often **add** on pullbacks to these levels.

---

## Entry Criteria

- **Trend alignment:** Only **long** when weekly/daily is in uptrend (HH/HL); only **short** when in downtrend (LH/LL). No counter-trend position trades unless you have a clear reversal thesis (e.g. Wyckoff distribution complete).
- **Pullback to level:** Price pulls back to **weekly** support (e.g. prior HL, 50 EMA, or 200 EMA) in an uptrend. **Confirmation:** Bullish weekly or daily candle (e.g. hammer, engulfing) or break of structure (BOS) back up. Enter on break of confirmation high or on next open.
- **Breakout:** Break of **major** range or pattern (e.g. multi-month consolidation) with **volume**. Enter on close above/below or on retest. Prefer breakouts **in the direction** of the higher-timeframe trend.

---

## Exit Criteria

- **Stop loss:** Below **major** structure (e.g. below the weekly HL that defined the pullback, or below the breakout level). Stops are **wide** (several percent or more); position size must be **small** so that one loss doesn’t hurt the account.
- **Take profit:** (1) **Next major resistance** (weekly/monthly). (2) **Trailing:** Trail stop under weekly swing lows (long); exit when price closes below the trail. (3) **Trend break:** Exit when the **trend** on your timeframe breaks (e.g. lower low in an uptrend).
- **Time:** Positions can be held for months; exit when **thesis** is invalid (trend break, target hit, or fundamental change).

---

## Stop Loss Placement

- **Structural:** Stop where the **position thesis** is wrong—e.g. below the key weekly low (long). Not a random percentage; use the chart.
- **Width:** Expect **2–5%** or more stop distance on daily/weekly. Size = Risk% / (Entry − Stop) so that dollar risk per trade is fixed (e.g. 1% of account).

---

## Risk:Reward Analysis

- **Target R:R:** **1:2 to 1:5** or more—position trades aim for **large** moves. Risk 1% per trade; target 2–5%+.
- **Position size:** With wide stops, **size is small**. e.g. 2% stop distance and 1% risk → position size = 0.5× account (50% of account in the trade if 1:1 notional). Adjust for leverage and instrument.

---

## Backtesting Suggestions

- **Data:** **Weekly** (and daily for entry logic); **many years** (10+ if available) to see different macro regimes.
- **Rules:** Define “uptrend” (e.g. price above 200 EMA and HH/HL on weekly). Entry = pullback to 50 EMA or prior HL with bullish weekly candle. Stop = below pullback low. Target = next weekly resistance or 2× risk.
- **Metrics:** Win rate, average R, max drawdown, number of trades per year (often 5–20). Position trading should have **low turnover** and **high average R** on winners.
- **Correlation:** If trading multiple instruments, account for **correlation** (e.g. long SPY and QQQ = overlapping risk).

# Section 3 — Candlestick Analysis

Candlestick charts show **open, high, low, close (OHLC)** in a visual form. Each candle reflects the battle between buyers and sellers in that period. **Patterns** (single, two, or three candles) can signal reversals or continuations—but **context** (trend, level, volume) is essential.

## Candle Anatomy

```
        Upper wick (high - max(open,close))
    │
    ●  ← Body (open to close; filled = down, empty = up in many platforms)
    │
        Lower wick (min(open,close) - low)
```

- **Body:** Open to close; size shows conviction.
- **Wicks (shadows):** Rejection or indecision at high/low.

---

## Key Principle

**Context matters:** A hammer at support in an uptrend is more meaningful than a hammer in the middle of a range. Always consider trend, key levels, and volume.

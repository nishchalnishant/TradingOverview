# Two Candlestick Patterns

Two-candle patterns use the **relationship between two consecutive candles** (body position, engulfing, etc.) to signal reversals or continuation. Context (trend, level) still matters.

---

## Bullish Engulfing

- **Shape:** First candle: bearish (red). Second candle: **bullish (green)** and its **body completely engulfs** the first candle’s body (open below first low, close above first high).
- **Meaning:** Strong shift from selling to buying. Buyers overwhelmed sellers. **Bullish** reversal signal.
- **Context:** At support, after a pullback in an uptrend, or after a downtrend. Stronger with **higher volume** on the engulfing candle.

```
  Candle 1 (bearish)   Candle 2 (bullish, engulfs 1)
       ███                  ███████
       ███                  ███████
                             ███████
```

- **Entry:** Long on break above the high of the engulfing candle. **Stop:** Below the low of the first (or second) candle.

---

## Bearish Engulfing

- **Shape:** First candle: bullish (green). Second candle: **bearish (red)** and its **body completely engulfs** the first candle’s body.
- **Meaning:** Sellers overwhelmed buyers. **Bearish** reversal signal.
- **Context:** At resistance, after a rally, or in an uptrend at a potential exhaustion point.

- **Entry:** Short on break below the low of the engulfing candle. **Stop:** Above the high of the second candle.

---

## Piercing Line

- **Shape:** First candle: bearish. Second candle: bullish; it **gaps down** (opens below first close), then closes **inside** the first candle’s body (above its midpoint) but **below** the first candle’s open.
- **Meaning:** Buyers stepped in after a gap down and recovered a good part of the loss. **Bullish** reversal, but weaker than full engulfing.
- **Context:** At support or after a decline.

- **Entry:** Long on confirmation (e.g. break above second candle high). **Stop:** Below second candle low.

---

## Dark Cloud Cover

- **Shape:** First candle: bullish. Second candle: bearish; it **gaps up** (opens above first high), then closes **inside** the first candle’s body (below its midpoint) but **above** the first candle’s open.
- **Meaning:** Sellers stepped in after a gap up and took back much of the gain. **Bearish** reversal.
- **Context:** At resistance or after a rally.

- **Entry:** Short on break below second candle low. **Stop:** Above second candle high.

---

## Tweezer Top

- **Shape:** Two candles (often one bullish, one bearish) with **same or nearly same high**. The second candle “rejects” the high. Looks like tweezers at the top.
- **Meaning:** Sellers defended the high. **Bearish** reversal signal at resistance or after a rally.
- **Context:** At resistance, at a LH, or after an up move.

```
      ●     ●   (same high)
     ███   ███
```

- **Entry:** Short on break below the low of the second candle. **Stop:** Above the common high.

---

## Tweezer Bottom

- **Shape:** Two candles with **same or nearly same low**. The second candle holds the low. **Bullish** reversal signal at support or after a decline.
- **Context:** At support, at a HL, or after a down move.

- **Entry:** Long on break above the high of the second candle. **Stop:** Below the common low.

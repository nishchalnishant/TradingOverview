# Candle Psychology and Context

## Candle Anatomy

- **Body:** Range from open to close. **Green/white** = close > open (bullish); **red/black** = close < open (bearish). Body **size** reflects conviction.
- **Upper wick:** High − max(open, close). Shows **rejection** at higher prices (sellers pushed price down from the high).
- **Lower wick:** min(open, close) − low. Shows **rejection** at lower prices (buyers pushed price up from the low).

## Candle Psychology

- **Large bullish body:** Strong buying; close near high = buyers in control.
- **Large bearish body:** Strong selling; close near low = sellers in control.
- **Small body (doji-like):** Indecision; open ≈ close. Can signal exhaustion or reversal when it appears after a strong move.
- **Long lower wick:** Sellers pushed price down, then buyers recovered—potential support/reversal signal at key levels.
- **Long upper wick:** Buyers pushed price up, then sellers rejected—potential resistance/reversal signal.

---

## Why Context Is Critical

- **Trend:** A hammer at **support in an uptrend** is a stronger bullish signal than a hammer after a long downtrend (could be a dead-cat bounce).
- **Level:** Candles at **support/resistance** or at **structure** (e.g. HL, HH) carry more weight than in the middle of nowhere.
- **Volume:** High volume on a reversal candle supports the signal; low volume suggests lack of conviction.
- **Sequence:** A single doji after one down day is weak; a doji after three large down candles at support is stronger (exhaustion).

---

## Trading Psychology

- **Pattern alone is not enough:** Avoid taking every hammer or doji; filter by level and trend to avoid overtrading and false signals.
- **Confirmation:** Wait for the **next candle** to confirm (e.g. bullish close after a hammer) before entering, to reduce false positives.

---

## Entry/Exit and Risk

- **Entry:** Use candle patterns as a **trigger** at a level you already identified (e.g. long on hammer at support; stop below hammer low).
- **Exit:** Target next resistance or use a risk:reward (e.g. 1:2). Trail stop once in profit.
- **Risk:** Place stop beyond the pattern’s extreme (e.g. below hammer low for longs).
